Definitions & Terms

Key concepts in Media and Information Languages (with examples)

Media Language

The way media communicates meaning through signs, symbols, and techniques.

Example: In a movie poster, dark colors and a shadowy figure suggest danger or mystery.

Denotation

The literal or direct meaning of a sign or image.

Example: A picture of a rose simply shows a flower.

Connotation

The implied or cultural meaning behind a sign or image.

Example: A rose may symbolize love, romance, or passion.

Mise-en-scène

Everything that appears in a film scene: setting, props, lighting, costume, and actors.

Example: A messy room, dark lighting, and torn clothes suggest chaos or danger.

Technical Codes

The technical aspects of media such as camera work, sound, lighting, and editing.

Example: A close-up shot makes the audience feel emotionally connected to a character.

Symbolic Codes

Meaning created through visual elements like color, body language, and setting.

Example: Red often symbolizes danger, anger, or love.

Written Codes

Any written text in media, including dialogue, captions, and lyrics.

Example: Subtitles in a film or slogans in advertisements.

Audience

The group of people that a media text is created for.

Example: A cartoon is usually created for children, while a horror film targets teens or adults.

Genre

A category of media that shares common characteristics.

Example: Horror films often include suspense, darkness, and scary music.

Conventions

Common rules or expectations in a type of media.

Example: In news programs, anchors usually sit behind a desk.
